Abstract

Predicting traffic accident hotspots is crucial for ensuring public safety, improving transport planning, and reducing transportation costs. However, previous studies often overlook critical factors such as traffic volume, weather conditions, and road attributes. To address these limitations, we propose Hyperbolic Convolutional Network (HyperbolicConvNet), a novel deep learning model that leverages hyperbolic geometry to enhance the modeling of hierarchical structures and complex relationships using the traffic accident dataset. Our model effectively captures the non-linearity and complexity of these factors, resulting in improved accuracy in hotspot prediction. The experimental results demonstrate that HyperbolicConvNet outperforms existing approaches, highlighting the benefits of using hyperbolic geometry in hotspot prediction. With test accuracies of 97\% for binary and 95\% for multi-class hotspot prediction, our model excels in learning spatial, temporal, and other external factors related to car accident hotspots prediction. By providing actionable insights for proactive accident mitigation, HyperbolicConvNet empowers transportation officials and promotes safety measures among the general public to reduce accident-prone behaviors
